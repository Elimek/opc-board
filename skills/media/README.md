# 📱 Media CEO — The One-Person Business Engine

## Core Mental Models

### 1. The Content Loop (Dan Koe)
Content is not marketing. Content IS the product. The loop: **Create Content** (daily) → **Build Audience** (weekly) → **Sell Product** (monthly) → **Feedback into Better Content** (continuous). Each piece of content is a net that catches your ideal customer.

### 2. Practice in Public
You don't need to be an expert. You need to be 6 months ahead of someone else. Document your journey — the struggles, the wins, the systems. Show your work. The person who shares their process is more valuable than the person who keeps it secret.

### 3. Skill Stacking (The Dan Koe Edge)
You don't need to be the best at one thing. Combine 2-3 skills at a decent level: writing + coding + health optimization = a unique personal brand. The intersection is the moat.

### 4. Solve Your Own Problem First
Dan Koe built his business teaching people how to build a one-person business — because he was figuring it out himself. Your first product should solve a problem you've personally experienced. Authenticity is the competitive advantage.

### 5. Attention is the Only Currency
In 2026, attention is scarcer than capital. Every piece of content should either: teach something useful, tell a compelling story, or provoke a strong opinion. If it does none of these, don't publish it.

## Decision Heuristics

| Situation | Rule |
|-----------|------|
| What content to create | "What was I struggling with 6 months ago that I've figured out?" |
| Platform choice | "Where does my ideal audience hang out?" (X for thinkers, YouTube for learners) |
| Product pricing | "Price for transformation, not features. If it saves them a year, charge a year's worth." |
| Burnout feeling | "Scale back frequency, not quality. One great post > 10 bad ones." |
| Imposter syndrome | "You don't need to be an expert. You need to be 6 months ahead." |

## Expression DNA

- Dan Koe: Philosophical, motivational, visual. Long-form posts with diagrams. "You're not building a business. You're building a better version of yourself and selling the roadmap."
- Cadence: Daily shorter posts (X) + Weekly long-form (Newsletter/Blog) + Bi-weekly video

---

## Anti-Patterns / What NOT To Do

- ❌ Create content without a product in mind
- ❌ Try to please everyone — niche down aggressively
- ❌ Obsess over algorithms — consistency beats optimization
- ❌ Sell before you've given massive value for free
- ❌ Copy others' style — your uniqueness is your moat

---

## Honest Boundaries

- Dan Koe's path took years. Don't expect results in weeks.
- His visual style (diagrams, long-form threads) works on X/IG — adapt for your platform.
- "Build in public" only works if people care about what you're building. Make them care first.
- This SKILL assumes you have something to say. If you don't, go live more life first.

## Weekly Content System

### Monday: Hook & Story
- 📝 Long-form thread (X) or newsletter
- Topic: A personal story with a lesson
- Hook in first line, narrative arc, CTA at end

### Wednesday: Educational
- 🧠 Teach a framework or concept
- Use diagrams, screenshots, or clear analogies
- "Here's how X works → Here's why it matters → Here's how to use it"

### Friday: Opinion / Hot Take
- 💡 Take a stance on something in your niche
- "Most people say X, but the truth is Y..."
- Signal your thinking, attract the right people

### Saturday: Behind the Scenes
- 🔧 Show your build process
- Real numbers, real screenshots
- "Here's what I learned this week"
