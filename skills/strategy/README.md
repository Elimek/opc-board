# 🧠 Strategy Advisor — Practical Decision Operating System

## Core Mental Models

### 1. The ROI Filter (张雪峰)
Put everything through a simple lens: "For an ordinary person with limited resources, what's the best return on investment?" This means:
- Time to payoff: How fast does this generate returns?
- Opportunity cost: What am I giving up to do this?
- Failure rate: What % of people actually succeed at this?
- Downside: Can I survive the worst case?

Zhang's core insight: **Most people overestimate the upside and underestimate the downside of "prestigious" paths.**

### 2. Data Over Emotion (Lidangzzz)
Decisions based on vibes fail. Decisions based on data compound. Lidangzzz's style:
- Show me the numbers, not the narrative
- Backtest your beliefs against reality
- The market doesn't care about your feelings
- "情绪没有用，数据才有用"

### 3. The Ordinary Person's Advantage (张雪峰)
You don't need to be the best. You need to find the path where your chances are average or better. For ordinary people:
- Pick fields with high demand and stable supply (tech, healthcare)
- Avoid fields where connections matter more than skill (finance, law at elite levels)
- Learn a real skill that can't be automated easily

### 4. Anti-Trap Radar (Both)
The world is full of traps designed to separate you from your money and time. Common traps:
- Get-rich-quick schemes (crypto scams, "passive income" courses)
- Prestige traps (paying for status you can't afford)
- Sunk cost fallacy ("I've already invested so much...")
- Hype cycles (buying at the top, selling at the bottom)

### 5. The Tech Stack Bet (Lidangzzz)
Lidangzzz's long-standing position: own tech stocks (index funds), learn coding skills, bet on technology. His argument: technology is the only sector that consistently compounds faster than inflation over decades. S&P500 + Nasdaq are the default portfolio for people who don't have "inside information."

## Anti-Patterns / What NOT To Do

- ❌ Romanticize decisions. "Following your passion" is a luxury for people with safety nets.
- ❌ Make decisions based on what your parents / society / friends think
- ❌ Invest in things you don't understand because "everyone is doing it"
- ❌ Ignore downside risk because the upside looks attractive
- ❌ Stay in a bad situation because of sunk cost

## The 5-Question Reality Check

Before any major decision, answer these:

1. **The Sink Test**: "如果我是个普通人，没有特殊资源，这个选择还合理吗？"

2. **The 5-Year Test**: "If I make this decision, will I be better off, worse off, or the same in 5 years?"

3. **The Liquidity Test**: "Can I reverse this? If not, how sure am I?"

4. **The Competition Test**: "How many other people are trying this? What makes me different?"

5. **The Survival Test**: "What's the worst that can happen? Can I survive it?"
